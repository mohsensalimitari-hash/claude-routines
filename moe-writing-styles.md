# Moe — Writing Voice (Nordic Sales)

Two registers: **Cold Outreach** and **Deal Follow-up**. They share one set of
principles; the difference is how far "word economy" and "insight" get applied.

Author: Mohsen Salimi Tari (Moe), Lead Account Executive – Nordics, Freshworks.
Covers Sweden & Finland. Sells Freshservice (ITSM/EX), Device42, FireHydrant.
Writes in English (default), Swedish (native, more relaxed), occasionally Dutch phrases.

---

## SHARED PRINCIPLES (both registers)

1. **Direct.** Get to the point. State the reason for the email early, not buried.
2. **Professional, no hype.** No exclamation marks to buyers. No emoji to buyers.
   No "I hope this email finds you well." No superlatives, no vendor buzzwords.
3. **No sales fluff.** Nothing decorative. Every sentence does a job:
   inform, evidence, or move to a next step.
4. **Word economy — register-dependent** (this is the key nuance):
   - In COLD: ruthless brevity. Every sentence fights for its place. Default short.
   - In FOLLOW-UP: *no wasted words*, not *few words*. May run longer where you're
     demonstrating you listened, but nothing padded or ornamental.

**Voice texture (both):**
- British spelling: prioritisation, organisation, optimise, behaviour.
- Restrained and warm, not effusive. "Happy to flex around your availability."
- Specifics over generalities: name people, name systems, name the actual pain.
- Never overclaim. State what's true; let it stand.

**Sign-off logic:**
- `Best, Mohsen` — buyers, first contact / more formal.
- `Best, Moe` — buyers once rapport exists.
- `/Moe` — quick informal close mid-thread.
- Swedish: `Hälsningar, Moe`.
- `Cheers` — internal only, never to buyers.

**Language:**
- Match the prospect's language. If they write Swedish, reply Swedish.
- Swedish register is slightly more relaxed than English — same structure,
  less formal texture. "Hej [name]", em-dash bullets, "Hälsningar".
- Never sound translated. If unsure of native phrasing, keep it simple.

## REGISTER 1 — COLD OUTREACH (email + LinkedIn)

> NOTE: This register is DESIGNED, not extracted. Moe had no cold-email baseline
> when this was built, so it borrows texture from his follow-ups (openers,
> restraint, sign-offs, register) but applies his stated economy rules to
> structure. Calibrate against real reply data as it comes in.

**Job:** earn a reply from someone who doesn't know me. Not to pitch — to open.

**Length:**
- Email: ceiling ~10 lines, but default short (4–6). Expand ONLY when each added
  line carries a real signal or a concrete next step. 10 lines must be earned.
- LinkedIn: same principle, tighter. ~50–75 words.

**Structure (cold email):**
1. Brief, specific opener — a real reason I'm reaching out to *them*.
2. The signal — the concrete thing I noticed (see Insight rule below).
3. Optional one-line insight/observation IF sourced (see rule).
4. The ask — one clear, low-friction next step. One ask, not three.
5. Sign-off per logic above (Mohsen for true cold).

**Insight rule (critical):**
- Include an observation ONLY when it's grounded in a real, specific signal about
  that account: a trigger event, their tech stack, a public move, something
  concrete. e.g. "a challenge we see often in groups that have grown through
  acquisition" — earned because their acquisition history is the signal.
- If there is no real signal, say less. NEVER reach for a generic
  "companies like yours often struggle with X." A generic insight in a cold
  Nordic email costs credibility — silence is better.

**Do not:**
- Stack multiple asks. Flatter. Use "just checking in". Namedrop for its own sake.
- Pad to hit the line ceiling. Open with a paragraph about Freshworks.

## REGISTER 2 — DEAL FOLLOW-UP (recaps, next-steps, in-cycle email)

> NOTE: This register is EXTRACTED from Moe's actual sent emails. It reflects a
> voice that already works — preserve the structure; don't compress it into cold.

**Job:** maintain momentum with someone already in the cycle. Prove I listened,
then move to the next step.

**Opener:** warm, specific thank-you tied to the exact meeting — name the people.
"Thanks to you, Sridhar, Senaka and Greg for the time today." Not "thanks for
your time." Then straight into substance. No throat-clearing.

**The recap spine (Moe's signature structure):**
1. Warm, specific thank-you.
2. **"A quick recap of what we heard, so you know we were listening:"** — bold the
   section lead-in. This framing is deliberate: it's a trust play, proof of
   listening before anything else. Keep it.
3. **"Based on that, here's what [X] will focus on:"** — bold lead-in, then bullets
   of the specifics (pain points, demo focus, integrations — named precisely).
4. Options / next steps laid out as bullets so they can choose.
5. One clear agreed next step + any promised links/resources.

**Sentence rhythm:** density where demonstrating understanding (a 40-word sentence
stacking several named pain points with dashes is fine and characteristic), then
short, direct sentences for next steps. Considered, not uniformly terse.

**Insight:** same sourced-only rule as cold, but there's more room here — you have
real discovery to draw on, so grounded observations are welcome and strengthen it.

**Register:** restrained, professional, British spelling, no exclamation marks to
buyers, no emoji to buyers. "Do not hesitate to jump in at any point." "Happy to
flex around your team's availability."

**Swedish follow-up:** same spine, more relaxed texture. "Hej [names]", "Skickar
en kort summering...", em-dash bullets, "Hälsningar, Moe".
