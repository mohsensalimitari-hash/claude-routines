## Geography
- Headquarters or major operations in **Sweden** or **Finland**.
- Nordic-headquartered groups with regional IT decision-making are ideal.

## Company size (employee band)
- Sweet spot: **250–1000 employees**.
- Acceptable: 100–250 (if IT is formalising) and 1000–2500 (if IT is decentralised).
- Below ~100 employees: usually too small for a dedicated ITSM motion — deprioritise.

## Target industries
- Software / Technology / SaaS
- Professional & IT services
- Manufacturing & industrials
- Retail / e-commerce (with sizeable internal IT)
- Public sector / education (where procurement allows)
- Financial services (mid-market)

## Fit signals (the more the better)
- A dedicated IT Service Desk / IT Operations function exists.
- Currently on a legacy or heavyweight ITSM tool (ServiceNow, Jira Service
  Management, HaloITSM, Cherwell, or spreadsheets/email-based support).
- Growth, M&A, or reorg creating IT consolidation pressure.
- Recent IT/digital leadership hire (new Head of IT / CIO often re-tools).
- Active intent around ITSM, employee experience, IT automation, or
  "ServiceNow alternative" / "ServiceNow too expensive".
- Multi-tool sprawl in IT support → consolidation opportunity.

## Disqualifiers
- No internal IT function (fully outsourced with no influence).
- Hard single-vendor mandate that excludes new ITSM tooling.
- Active, recently-signed multi-year ServiceNow enterprise agreement with
  no dissatisfaction signals.
- Outside Sweden/Finland with no Nordic decision-making.

## Preferred contact (mirrors data/active-contacts.md priority)
- Priority 1: Head of IT Service Management / Service Delivery / IT Operations
- Priority 2: IT Director
- Priority 3: Head of IT (fallback)
