## Where to focus first

### Size (primary filter)
- **Sweet spot: 1,000–5,000 employees.** This is the main focus band.
- **Outside the band only on very strong intent.** Sub-1,000 or 5,000–20,000+
  are pursued *only* when there is a clear, funded trigger (see below) strong
  enough to justify the exception. Larger accounts are winnable but need
  tighter discovery and a clear displacement/modernisation story.

### Industries (strongest first)
1. **Manufacturing**
2. **Financial Services**
3. Business Services
4. Wholesale & Distribution
5. Healthcare / Pharma
6. Retail

### Be selective
- **Public sector / government / education** — historically very hard to win in
  the Nordics (procurement frameworks, data-residency). Not impossible, but do
  not make them a lead focus.
- **Exception:** commercially-run, state-owned enterprises (e.g. Svevia) behave
  like private companies — fair game.

## The buying triggers (the #1 predictor of a deal)

An **active, funded project with a timeline** is the single biggest predictor.
Look for:
- A tool being retired / replaced.
- A merger or acquisition (integration pressure).
- Rapid growth outpacing IT.
- An audit or compliance driver.
- A service-desk overhaul / "build ITSM properly for the first time."

**Recognised pain without an active project** is a weaker but valid opening:
growing IT team with no self-service, a legacy tool that hasn't kept pace, or
an org that scaled fast without ever formalising IT service management.

> Rule of thumb: right size + right industry but **no active project = nurture,
> not pursue**. The best deals pair a good-fit company with a real, funded
> reason to act now.

## Incumbent tools = displacement signal

Currently running one of these is a strong fit signal (they've likely outgrown
it or find it too costly/complex):

- **HaloITSM** — *elevated for the Nordics; very active competitor here.*
- **ServiceNow** — high maturity, but cost/complexity pain ("more for less").
- **TOPdesk** — common, but weight slightly lower in the Nordics than the
  NL-skewed playbook implies.
- **BMC**, **ManageEngine**, **Ivanti**, **Jira Service Management**, or an
  **ageing legacy / free ticketing** tool.

Two maturity bands:
- **Low maturity** (TOPdesk / ManageEngine / legacy): ticket-based, little/no
  AI, no CMDB. Conversation = "what proper ITSM could unlock without breaking
  the budget."
- **Higher maturity** (ServiceNow / BMC): ambition is there but cost is high and
  budget/resources don't stretch. Conversation = "get more for less."

Supporting signal in both: **specialised IT roles** (IT Operations, Security)
indicate an org ready to run real ITSM.

## Who to talk to (persona set)

| Persona | Cares about | Opening question |
|---|---|---|
| **IT Leader** (CIO / VP / Director of IT) — budget owner | Cost, modernisation, ROI | "How many tools are you running for service, assets and operations — and how many people just to administer them?" |
| **Head of IT Service Delivery / Service Management** — usual champion | Ticket volume, SLAs, agent productivity, self-service | "How has ticket volume changed in 12–18 months relative to team size — and how sustainable is that?" |
| **Infrastructure & Operations Leader** | Uptime, infrastructure, CMDB, assets | "Do you have a single source of truth for assets — or is the CMDB maintained by hand?" |

Target **3–4 contacts per company**, and aim to cover **the budget owner + the
champion** among them.

## Why Freshservice wins (lead with what matches the pain)

- **Unified platform** — ITSM, ITOM, ITAM and ESM on one platform with a common
  CMDB; one service model serves IT, HR, Finance. Strengthened by **Device42**
  (asset intelligence) and **FireHydrant** (incident response).
- **Domain-specialised AI (Freddy)** — ~45 AI service workflows trained on a
  decade of service data. Benchmarks: **65%+ query deflection, ~40% agent
  productivity lift, 77% reduction in average resolution time** with AI assist.
- **Rapid impact** — **80% of customers live in under 90 days**, no-code setup,
  independent ease-of-deployment **4.6/5** (ahead of ServiceNow and Atlassian).
  Ships **updates monthly** vs. ServiceNow's ~3×/year.
- **Trusted at scale** — public, transparent pricing (no module surprises),
  built-in security/compliance/AI guardrails, ITIL-certified, strong Forrester
  Wave ESM performer.

> Proof figures are platform benchmarks, not guarantees for any single customer.
> Only ever cite these numbers — never invent metrics.

## Competitor positioning (how to frame Freshservice)

- **HaloITSM** *(watch closely in the Nordics)* — competes on SLA tooling and
  configurability; the gap opens on **AI and platform breadth**. Freshservice
  ships a no-code AI agent studio with pre-built IT workflows out of the box;
  Halo's AI requires customers to connect/manage their own Azure OpenAI. On
  ITAM, Freshservice bundles discovery, dependency mapping and CMDB as one
  product; Halo needs a separately licensed third-party discovery tool.
- **ServiceNow** — powerful but complex and consultant-heavy. Win on
  **time-to-value** (live in weeks, not quarters), **TCO**, and no army of
  specialists to make a change.
- **TOPdesk** — a traditional service-desk tool. Position on **AI depth,
  ITOM/ITAM breadth, and a modern unified platform**.
- **Jira Service Management** — a developer tool stretched into ITSM. Position on
  **purpose-built ITIL processes**, no-code ease for non-technical teams, and
  easy expansion beyond IT into HR/Finance/Facilities.

## Good-fit checklist (score a prospect fast)

- ☐ 1,000–5,000 employees? (Outside the band → only with very strong intent.)
- ☐ Manufacturing, Financial Services, Wholesale, Healthcare or Retail?
- ☐ **Active, funded project with a timeline?** (the #1 signal) — or visible
  friction with the current setup?
- ☐ Outgrowing / frustrated with a current tool (HaloITSM, ServiceNow, TOPdesk,
  Jira, legacy)?
- ☐ Low-maturity (TOPdesk/ManageEngine/legacy) **or** cost-constrained on
  ServiceNow/BMC?
- ☐ Specialised IT roles (IT Operations, Security) present?
- ☐ NOT mainly public sector / education with no compelling trigger.
